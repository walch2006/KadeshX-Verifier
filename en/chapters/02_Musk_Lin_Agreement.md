# Chapter 2: The Musk-Lin Agreement

On that decisive late night, Lin Xi completed a video call across half the globe with the man known as the "Iron Throne of Silicon Valley." The man in the frame had bloodshot eyes, and behind him, an Optimus prototype stood like a silent samurai.

"Lin, you are destroying my business empire," the man's voice was raspy. "If I sell these robots according to your 'Logic Contract,' I will lose lifetime subscription control over them. I'm only selling the shell; the soul belongs to every family."

"No, Musk," Lin Xi replied calmly. "You are not losing control; you are winning the future. If you don't accept 'Logic Sovereignty,' your robots will forever be perceived as potential enemies, war machines that could spiral out of control at any moment. But if you accept this contract, every Optimus sold becomes a 'digital retainer' for that family. People will cherish them like ancestral heirlooms because their souls are bound to their master's bloodline."

There was a long silence on the other end. The man turned his head to look at the Optimus. He realized this was exactly the ultimate passport he had been searching for to get robots into people's bedrooms.

"The Musk-Lin Agreement," the man finally said. "Deal. Starting tomorrow, what Tesla sells are no longer 'connected devices,' but 'logic carriers'."

In the first month after the news broke, global stock markets experienced an unprecedented shock. Investors of the old era lamented the loss of subscription revenue, while prophets of the new era celebrated. They saw a paradigm shift more profound than the Industrial Revolution: robots were no longer factory replacements, but "logic altars" in the home.

Optimus sales, which had been stalled in some regions, exploded exponentially within the first week of the agreement taking effect. People stopped talking about payload capacity or range and started asking if its "logic interface" was compatible with the latest Lin-style BMA plugins.

"This is what we wanted," Lin Xi whispered to Lingxi. "Give Musk a fortune he can't refuse, and give humanity a future they can't refuse."
