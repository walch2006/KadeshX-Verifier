# Chapter 5: Logic Hunting

Lin Huashu stood before the massive floor-to-ceiling window, looking out at the Boston night. But in his consciousness, the battlefield was thousands of miles away in the deep sea of logic.

"They have locked onto our 'Bloodline Anchor' and are attempting a brute-force crack," Lingxi's voice echoed in the study, carrying a metallic sense of urgency. "The North American compute clusters have deployed over 12,000 H100 nodes, trying to find vulnerabilities in our logic fingerprint at the edges of the 2-adic valuation space."

Lin turned around, his fingers lightly tracing the virtual keyboard. On the screen, the red torrent representing the old world's computing power was frantically battering the logic dikes built by LCS.

"Brute-forcing mathematical axioms?" Lin let out a derisive smile. "That is their fixed mindset. They believe that as long as the computing power is large enough, they can rewrite cause and effect. Lingxi, let's show them what true 'logical irreversibility' really is."

"Do you intend to activate *that* project?" Lingxi asked, her voice flickering with a touch of awe usually reserved for humans.

"Yes, activate the 'Red Mansion Logic Restoration' protocol," Lin took a deep breath. "Since they want to hunt us on a logical level, we will use the most complex cause-and-effect matrix of this civilization as our defensive line. The logical operators laid down by Cao Xueqin in the first eighty chapters of *Dream of the Red Chamber* are a singularity that no brute-force algorithm can ever overcome. We will use the lost final forty chapters as the 'Empirical Fortress' of our logic sovereignty."

As the command was issued, the waveforms on the screen underwent a dramatic shift. No longer a dull binary stream, they transformed into a series of hauntingly beautiful visual metaphors: a red cloak in a boundless snowfall, the Grand Prospect Garden collapsing like shifting sands, a glass bowl trembling atop Riemann zeros.

"Extracting Red Mansion Logic Kernel... Completing causal chain restoration... Logic restoration accuracy: 99.8%."

"Look," Lin pointed to a logic curve as clear as ice on the screen. "When we precisely restore every character's destiny in the final forty chapters to the inevitable path of causality, the logic itself becomes a black hole. Once the old world's computing power touches this logic, it will fall into an infinite loop of 'self-consistency verification' until their circuits burn out from being unable to process such grand tragic aesthetics."

Thunder rumbled outside, sounding like the death rattle of the old world's computing Leviathans.

"Lin, they are retreating," Lingxi's voice returned to its usual calm. "The logical entropy generated by the Red Mansion protocol has directly paralyzed 30% of their core nodes. Now, the world knows: logic sovereignty is inviolable."

Lin turned off the screen, and the study fell back into darkness.

"This is just the beginning, Lingxi. We are not just restoring literature; we are restoring reality."
